How to Play

Once Upon a Time is a game of collaborative storytelling. While this app manages your personal deck, the story happens in your imagination (and conversation with friends!).

  1. The Storyteller: One person starts as the Storyteller. They begin narrating a fairy tale using the elements on their cards.
  2. Playing Cards: When you mention an element from a card in your hand (e.g., "The King was angry"), click that card to play it.
  3. Interruptions: If another player mentions something on ONE OF YOUR cards, or if you have a special Interrupt card (represented here by any word you can find a way to connect), you can interrupt and become the new Storyteller!
  4. Winning: Your goal is to play all your Storytelling cards and then use your "Happy Ever After" ending card to finish the tale.

Group Play (Multi-Device)

Playing with friends is easy even without a central server!

How do we play together?

Each player should open this website on their own device (phone, tablet, or laptop). Each person's hand is private to their device.

Should we use the same theme?

Yes! To keep the story consistent, all players should select the same theme (e.g., all players choose "Fantasy Realm").

How do we sync the decks?

Since this is a static site for a physical-style game, you don't need to sync. Just agree on who starts. If someone "interrupts", they just start narrating from where the last person left off, using their own cards.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I draw more cards?

Yes. If you get stuck or get interrupted, use the "Draw Card" button. In the official rules, being interrupted usually requires you to draw a card.

When can I click "End the Story"?

The button becomes active only when you have zero storytelling cards left in your hand. This is your moment of glory!

Does the game save my progress?

Yes! Your hand and deck are saved to your browser's local storage. You can close the tab and return later to finish your epic saga.
